Who I Am

Rooted in Justice.
Driven by People.

With years of dedicated work at the intersection of criminal justice and community safety in Kenya, Beryl Akinyi has become a trusted voice for reform, rehabilitation, and systemic change. Her work spans programme management, crisis intervention, policy advocacy, and grassroots community outreach.

She believes that real safety is built not through enforcement alone, but through trust, equity, and the active involvement of every community member in shaping their own future.

Featured Policy Reform

[Intensive Probation and Reintegration Program]

My Daily Reality In my role at Makadara Probation, I occupy the fragile space between punishment and redemption. I have stood in the doorways of Kayole's dense settlements, knocked on corrugated iron doors, and sat on plastic stools in dimly lit rooms to verify that a manslaughter offender is indeed rebuilding his life as a boda boda mechanic. I have walked through police station corridors where the smell of disinfectant cannot mask desperation, conducting presentence inquiries while suspects still wear the handcuffs of their arrest—some resigned, others still bargaining for innocence. These field studies are not bureaucratic exercises; they are my method of grounding court orders in the messy truth of people's actual lives. My Caseload Spectrum
