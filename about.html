Who I Am

Advocate. Manager.
Community Builder.

Beryl Akinyi is a criminal justice management professional based in Nairobi, Kenya, with deep experience working at the intersection of policy, advocacy, and community safety. Her journey began not in a classroom, but on the streets of her own community — where she witnessed firsthand the gap between the justice system's promise and its lived reality.

That gap became her life's work. From crisis intervention in underserved Nairobi neighbourhoods to crafting reform-focused policy recommendations presented to local government, Beryl has consistently operated at the frontlines of change — not from a distance, but from within the communities she serves.

She holds a deep conviction that sustainable community safety cannot be enforced from the outside in. It must be cultivated from the inside out built on trust, dignity, and the active participation of every voice in a community, especially those that have historically been silenced.

What Drives Me

Core Values &
Principles

⚖️
01

Equity Over Equality

True justice means giving people what they need not just treating everyone the same. Every policy and programme Beryl champions centres on closing systemic gaps.

🤝
02

Community First

No intervention succeeds without community buy-in. Beryl's approach always begins with listening deeply, honestly, and without agenda before any action is taken.

🔍
03

Evidence-Led Advocacy

Passion without data is noise. Every initiative is grounded in rigorous research, measurable outcomes, and honest evaluation of what works and what doesn't.

🌱
04

Rehabilitation Over Punishment

People are more than their worst moments. Beryl advocates for systems that invest in rehabilitation, reintegration, and second chances as the foundation of lasting safety.

The Person
Behind the Purpose

Outside of her professional work, Beryl is a mentor, a reader, and an avid community volunteer. She believes the best advocates are those who remain deeply human who can sit with someone in pain, celebrate small victories, and hold space for the full complexity of people's lives.
